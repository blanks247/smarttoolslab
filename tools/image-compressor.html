Understanding Image Compression: Lossy vs Lossless

There are two main types of image compression: Lossy and Lossless. Our tool primarily uses lossy compression for JPG and WebP files, which intelligently removes data that the human eye can't easily perceive. This allows for massive reductions in file size (often up to 90%) with minimal impact on quality. For PNG files, we focus on optimizing the color palette and removing unnecessary metadata to maintain transparency while reducing weight.

By using the "Increase Quality" mode, you can minimize data loss for professional photos, while "Decrease Size" is optimized for web performance and form submissions.

Technical Insight: In-Browser Processing

SmartToolsLab leverages the Canvas API and the browser's native image rendering engine. When you upload an image, we draw it onto a hidden canvas element. Then, we use the toBlob() method with specific quality parameters to re-encode the image. This method is incredibly fast and ensures that your private photos never leave your device, making it one of the most secure ways to compress images online.

Frequently Asked Questions

How to compress image to 50KB for SSC form?

Upload your photo, select "Decrease Size", and set the Max Width to around 800px. This usually brings most photos under the 50KB or 20KB limits required for government applications.

Does this tool support bulk compression?

Currently, we focus on providing the highest quality per-image optimization. You can process images one by one very quickly. Bulk support is coming soon!

Are my images safe?

Absolutely. Your safety is our priority. Since all processing happens locally in your browser, your files never leave your device. We don't store or see your images.

What image formats are supported?

We support all major web formats, including JPG, JPEG, PNG, and WebP. You can even convert between formats by choosing your desired output settings.
